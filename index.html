What Dog Loves

  • Exercise and Play: Dogs love to run, fetch, and play. Regular exercise is important for their physical and mental health.
  • Treats and Food: Most dogs are highly motivated by food. Treats are often used as a reward for training and good behavior.
  • Companionship: Dogs are pack animals and love spending time with their human family. They enjoy being involved in family activities and don't like being left alone for long periods.
  • Toys: From chew toys to puzzle toys, dogs enjoy having something to play with. Toys can also help keep them mentally stimulated.

What Dog Hate

  1. Being Left Alone: Dogs are social animals and usually don't like being left alone for long periods. They may become anxious or bored.
  2. Loud Noises: Thunderstorms, fireworks, and other loud noises can be frightening for dogs. They often seek comfort during these times.
  3. Unfamiliar Places and People: New environments and strangers can make some dogs anxious or nervous.
  4. Rough Handling: Dogs generally dislike being handled roughly or in a way that causes discomfort, such as tugging on their ears or tail.
  5. Heat and Cold: Extreme temperatures can be uncomfortable and even dangerous for dogs. They prefer moderate climates.

Breed Of Dogs For Security purpose:Descending List From High To low

  1. German Shepherd: Known for their intelligence, strength, and loyalty, German Shepherds are often used in police and military work
  2. Doberman Pinscher: Highly intelligent and alert, Dobermans are known for their loyalty and protective nature
  3. Rottweiler: Powerful and confident, Rottweilers are fiercely loyal to their families and make excellent guard dogs
  4. Bullmastiff: Large and intimidating, Bullmastiffs are natural protectors and are known for their gentle nature with family members
  5. Belgian Malinois: Athletic and highly trainable, Belgian Malinois are favorites in K-9 units for their agility and protective instincts
  6. Cane Corso: A large Italian breed, Cane Corsos are known for their guarding abilities and loyalty
  7. Akita: Loyal and courageous, Akitas are natural protectors and take their guarding duties seriously
  8. Komondor: Known for their unique corded coat, Komondors are powerful and protective, originally bred to guard livestock
  9. Boxer: Energetic and alert, Boxers are protective of their families and make good watchdogs
  10. Great Dane: Despite their gentle nature, Great Danes are imposing and can be protective of their homes
